The ShoeMaker Pages

For 47 years, he’s mended soles — and souls — one step at a time. In a tiny workshop on a busy Ethiopian street, an old shoemaker quietly studies the shoes of his customers, reading the wear and tear like chapters in a book. But when the problems he sees in their lives grow too big for leather and thread, he makes a bold decision: to become a voice for the voiceless… by running for the House of Peoples’ Representatives.

This powerful film, rooted in humanity and resilience, has not only touched the hearts of local audiences but also received international recognition for its storytelling and impact. We are honored to have been acknowledged by esteemed film festivals around the world:
